Rolls-Royce Nuclear Engineering Summer Workshop at Leeds University

2018

Undertook a 4 day long workshop in Nuclear Engineering at Leeds University. During this time I learnt a lot about Nuclear Engineering and the nuclear industry in general. One of our projects was to work together and see how Rolls-Royce submarines are designed and how they can have nuclear reactors on board. In particular, our group focused on Small Modular Reactors (SMR's) which are in essence small nuclear reactors that can be fit onto submarines. We also met professionals from Rolls-Royce who told us about the future of Nuclear Engineering and what working in industry is like.

Skills gained:

  • Teamwork: Worked in teams throughout the entire workshop. Our teams main focus was Small Modular Reactors and two of our projects included making a presentation and giving it infront of faculty, professionals and other students and looking at different parts of submarines and what tools and techniques engineers use to make them suited for their tasks. For example, we saw ways of cooling a SMR on board a submarine by seeing a small scale demonstration.
  • Communication: Being in a group with a lot of different people who came from different places meant I had to be able to communicate effectively with them. Good communication with other members led to our team working efficiently and progressing quickly on our projects.
  • Leadership: I was the project leader of a presentation on Small Modular Reactors (SMR's). As group leader, I organised and assigned roles to the rest of the team and implemented techniques to help our team run smoothly and efficiently. One of the techniques I used was to use Slack as a team message board to help us communicate with each other easily and organise meetings to discuss the presentation.
